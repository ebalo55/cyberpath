## Overview

The **Phishing and Spear-Phishing Bootcamp (PSP)** is designed to equip cybersecurity professionals with the skills
necessary to recognize, respond to, and mitigate phishing threats. Offered by the MCSI, this bootcamp provides hands-on
training in identifying and defending against these prevalent cyber attacks.

The bootcamp covers essential techniques such as:

- Reconnaissance and social engineering tactics.
- Email spoofing and website cloning methods.
- Payload creation and defense evasion techniques.

## Course Details

This bootcamp emphasizes practical exercises that simulate real-world phishing scenarios. Participants will learn how to
effectively counteract phishing attempts and develop strategies for organizational defense.

### Topics Covered:

- Understanding the mechanics of phishing and spear-phishing attacks.
- Techniques for recognizing malicious emails and links.
- Strategies for educating users on phishing awareness.
- Tools for monitoring and mitigating phishing threats.

## Exam Structure

Participants will engage in practical assessments throughout the bootcamp to evaluate their understanding and
application of the techniques learned.

### Key Exam Requirements:

- Participation in hands-on simulation exercises.
- Demonstration of knowledge in identifying phishing attempts.
- Development of a comprehensive defense strategy against phishing attacks.

## Who Should Take PSP?

The PSP bootcamp is ideal for:

- Aspiring Blue Team Specialists focused on incident response.
- Cybersecurity Managers looking to enhance their team's awareness of phishing threats.
- Risk Management Specialists aiming to develop effective mitigation strategies.
- Threat Hunters seeking to improve their detection capabilities against social engineering attacks.
