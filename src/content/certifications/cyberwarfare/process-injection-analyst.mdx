## Overview

The **Certified Process Injection Analyst (CPIA)** certification is designed for individuals looking to deepen their
understanding of advanced process injection techniques and their detection. This certification equips both red and blue
team professionals with the knowledge required to analyze and respond to process injection attacks effectively.

- Understand various advanced process injection techniques.
- Identify telemetry generated from these techniques.
- Conduct event analysis of collected footprints and reduce operational footprints during post-exploitation.

## Course Details

The CPIA certification includes a structured course that caters to both beginners and intermediate practitioners in
cybersecurity.

### Topics Covered:

- **Advanced Process Techniques**: In-depth exploration of process injection methods.
- **Telemetry Identification**: Techniques for recognizing telemetry associated with injection attacks.
- **Event Analysis**: Analyzing collected data to understand attack vectors.
- **Post-Exploitation Footprint Management**: Strategies for minimizing detection during operations.

## Exam Structure

The CPIA certification is awarded upon successful completion of a practical exam that assesses the candidate's ability
to apply learned techniques in realistic scenarios.

### Key Exam Requirements:

- Completion of all course modules.
- Participation in hands-on labs with access to simulated environments.

## Who Should Take CPIA?

The CPIA certification is ideal for:

- Red teamers seeking to enhance their offensive capabilities.
- Blue teamers wanting to improve their detection and response strategies.
- Security operations specialists looking to understand advanced attack methodologies.
