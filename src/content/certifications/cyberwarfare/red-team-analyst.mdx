## Overview

The **Certified Red Team Analyst (CRTA)** certification is designed for individuals looking to develop their skills in
red teaming and offensive security. This certification provides a comprehensive understanding of the methodologies and
tools used in red team operations, enabling candidates to simulate real-world cyber attacks effectively.

- Set up and execute red team operations.
- Conduct both external and internal offensive maneuvers.
- Analyze and report on security weaknesses within an organization.

## Course Details

The CRTA certification encompasses a structured course divided into five key sections, ensuring a well-rounded education
in red teaming practices.

### Topics Covered:

- **Introduction to Red Teaming**: Fundamental concepts and objectives of red teaming.
- **Red Team Lab Setup**: Practical guidance on establishing a red team environment.
- **External Offensive Operations**: Techniques for attacking from outside an organization.
- **Internal Offensive Operations**: Strategies for navigating and exploiting internal networks.
- **Practical Case Study**: Hands-on lab experience to apply learned skills in realistic scenarios.

## Exam Structure

The CRTA certification is awarded upon successful completion of a practical assessment that evaluates the candidate's
ability to perform red team operations. Candidates will engage in simulated attack scenarios that require critical
thinking and technical skills.

### Key Exam Requirements:

- Completion of all course modules.
- Successful demonstration of offensive techniques in a lab environment.

## Who Should Take CRTA?

The CRTA certification is ideal for:

- Aspiring red team specialists seeking foundational knowledge.
- Penetration testers wanting to expand their offensive capabilities.
- Security professionals interested in understanding adversarial tactics.
