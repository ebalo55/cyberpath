## Overview

The **ISC2 CISO Leadership Certificates (CISO)** are designed to help cybersecurity professionals advance their careers
and excel in executive leadership roles. These certificates provide a pathway to the ISC2 CISSP certification while
demonstrating a commitment to maintaining your skills through professional development.

The CISO certificates validate an individual's ability to:

- Lead high-performing cybersecurity teams.
- Gain support for security initiatives.
- Manage incident response and recovery.

## Course Details

The CISO bundle includes four key certificates:

1. **Building a High-Performing Cybersecurity Team**: Learn how to build and manage a talented cybersecurity team
   through best practices in hiring and recruitment.

2. **Purple Team Playbook**: Understand the roles and responsibilities of red and blue teams, and how to combine them
   into an effective purple team.

3. **Gaining Support for Your Security Program**: Manage stakeholder perspectives and perceptions to win support for
   your security initiatives.

4. **Incident Management: Preparation and Response**: Prepare for and respond to security incidents, leveraging them as
   opportunities to improve your organization's security posture.

### Key Benefits:

- Pathway to ISC2 CISSP certification.
- Demonstrate commitment to maintaining your ISC2 certification.
- Stay current with emerging cybersecurity leadership topics.
- Earn CPE credits for maintaining your ISC2 certifications.

## Exam Structure

To earn each CISO certificate, candidates must score 70% or higher on the final assessment. Learners have the
flexibility to complete the full course or go directly to the assessment if they already possess the necessary
knowledge.

### Key Exam Requirements:

- Passing score of 70% or higher on the final assessment.
- Option to complete learning modules or take assessment only.

## Who Should Take CISO?

The CISO certificates are ideal for:

- Cybersecurity managers looking to advance their leadership skills.
- Risk management specialists aiming for executive roles.
- Security operations specialists focused on incident management.
