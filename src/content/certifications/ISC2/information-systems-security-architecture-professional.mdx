## Overview

The **Certified Information Systems Security Architecture Professional (ISSAP)** certification is designed for
experienced information security professionals who want to demonstrate their expertise in designing and implementing
security solutions. Offered by ISC2, this certification validates an individual's ability to provide risk-based guidance
to senior management and develop security architectures that align with organizational goals.

The certification validates an individualâ€™s ability to:

- Develop, design, and analyze security solutions across an organization.
- Provide governance, compliance, and risk management strategies.
- Architect security solutions that protect critical information assets.

## Course Details

The ISSAP certification encompasses six domains that cover essential aspects of security architecture.

### Topics Covered:

- **Governance, Compliance, and Risk Management**: Architecting frameworks that ensure compliance and manage risks
  effectively.
- **Security Architecture Modeling**: Techniques for modeling security architectures to meet organizational needs.
- **Infrastructure Security Architecture**: Designing secure infrastructure to protect against threats.
- **Identity and Access Management (IAM) Architecture**: Implementing robust IAM systems to control access to resources.
- **Application Security Architecture**: Architecting secure applications throughout their lifecycle.
- **Security Operations Architecture**: Establishing operational frameworks for effective security management.

### Learning Materials:

Participants will have access to a variety of resources, including official training materials, practice exams, and
community support from experienced security architects.

## Exam Structure

The ISSAP certification is awarded upon successful completion of the exam that assesses knowledge across the covered
domains.

### Key Exam Requirements:

- A minimum of two years of cumulative paid work experience in one or more of the six domains of the ISSAP Common Body
  of Knowledge (CBK).
- The exam consists of multiple-choice questions covering all domains.

## Who Should Take ISSAP?

The ISSAP certification is ideal for:

- Cybersecurity managers responsible for overseeing security architecture initiatives.
- Risk management specialists aiming to enhance their understanding of security architecture principles.
- Security operations specialists looking to integrate architectural considerations into operational practices.
