## Overview

The **ISC2 Risk Management Practitioner Certificates (RMPC)** are designed to equip cybersecurity professionals with the
skills necessary to effectively assess and manage risks within their organizations. These certificates provide a
comprehensive understanding of risk management principles, methodologies, and standards essential for success in today’s
cybersecurity landscape.

The certification validates an individual’s ability to:

- Conduct practical risk assessments and analyses.
- Understand and apply international risk standards.
- Utilize effective risk management methods to prioritize and mitigate risks.

## Course Details

The RMPC bundle includes three key certificates:

1. **Conducting Practical Risk Analysis Certificate**: This course focuses on hands-on experience with risk assessment,
   analysis, mitigation, and remediation strategies. Participants will learn how to holistically examine operational
   risk management and communicate findings effectively.

2. **Exploring Risk Standards Certificate**: This course provides an overview of international risk standards, enhancing
   participants' understanding of compliance and governance in risk management across various industries.

3. **Practical Risk Methods Certificate**: This course reviews techniques for comparing and measuring risks, emphasizing
   qualitative and quantitative methods for effective risk prioritization and assessment.

### Topics Covered:

- Risk assessment processes and methodologies.
- International standards for risk management.
- Techniques for effective communication of risk findings.

## Exam Structure

To earn each RMPC certificate, candidates must score 70% or higher on the final assessment. Participants can choose to
complete the full course or go directly to the assessment if they already possess the necessary knowledge.

### Key Exam Requirements:

- Passing score of 70% or higher on the final assessment.
- Option to complete learning modules or take assessment only.

## Who Should Take RMPC?

The RMPC is ideal for:

- Risk management specialists looking to deepen their expertise.
- Cybersecurity managers seeking to implement effective risk strategies.
- Security operations specialists focused on managing organizational risks.
