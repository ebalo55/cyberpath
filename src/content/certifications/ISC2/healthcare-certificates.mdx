## Overview

The **ISC2 Healthcare Certificates (HC)** are designed to equip cybersecurity professionals with the knowledge and
skills necessary to protect sensitive patient information and navigate the complexities of healthcare regulations. These
certificates provide a pathway to enhance your expertise in securing healthcare environments.

The certification validates an individualâ€™s ability to:

- Understand the unique challenges of healthcare information security.
- Implement effective privacy and security measures in healthcare settings.
- Conduct risk management and assessments specific to healthcare organizations.

## Course Details

The HC bundle includes three key certificates:

1. **Healthcare Essentials: Information Security in Healthcare Settings**: This foundational course covers essential
   concepts related to healthcare security, including data management and privacy challenges.

2. **Privacy and Security for Healthcare Organizations**: Focused on security and privacy principles, this course
   explores sensitive data handling and the interaction between security and privacy in healthcare environments.

3. **Risk Management and Risk Assessment in a Healthcare Setting**: This course builds knowledge in enterprise risk
   management, control assessments, and mitigation strategies tailored for healthcare settings.

### Topics Covered:

- Fundamentals of healthcare information security.
- Security principles specific to healthcare organizations.
- Risk management processes and unique applications in healthcare.

## Exam Structure

To earn each HC certificate, candidates must score 70% or higher on the final assessment. Learners can choose to
complete the full course or go directly to the assessment if they already possess the necessary knowledge.

### Key Exam Requirements:

- Passing score of 70% or higher on the final assessment.
- Option to complete learning modules or take assessment only.

## Who Should Take HC?

The HC certificates are ideal for:

- Cybersecurity managers looking to specialize in healthcare security.
- Risk management specialists aiming to address risks in healthcare settings.
- Security operations specialists focused on protecting patient data.
