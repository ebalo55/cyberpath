## Overview

The **ISC2 Executive Leadership Certificates (ELC)** are designed to empower cybersecurity professionals with the
leadership skills necessary to navigate the complexities of cybersecurity governance and strategy. These courses provide
actionable insights and strategies that enable professionals to effectively communicate with stakeholders and lead their
organizations in an evolving threat landscape.

The certification validates an individual’s ability to:

- Communicate cybersecurity risks and strategies to the board of directors.
- Lead incident response efforts in collaboration with executive leadership.
- Present complex cybersecurity concepts in a business-friendly manner.

## Course Details

The ELC bundle includes three key courses:

1. **Cybersecurity for the Board of Directors**: This course teaches how to build productive relationships with board
   members, focusing on effective communication regarding incidents, risks, and security strategies.

2. **The Board of Directors’ Role in Incident Response**: This course explores the responsibilities of the board during
   incidents, emphasizing organizational liabilities and effective collaboration for incident management.

3. **Presenting to Your Board of Directors**: This course provides techniques for creating engaging presentations that
   translate technical cybersecurity language into terms that resonate with board members.

### Topics Covered:

- Strategies for effective communication with executive leadership.
- Incident response roles and responsibilities.
- Storytelling techniques for impactful presentations.

## Exam Structure

Each course is delivered on-demand, allowing participants to complete them at their own pace. Participants earn CPE
credits upon completion, reinforcing their commitment to ongoing professional development.

### Key Exam Requirements:

- Completion of each course module.
- Participation in interactive elements and case studies.

## Who Should Take ELC?

The ELC is ideal for:

- Cybersecurity managers seeking to enhance their leadership skills.
- Risk management specialists aiming to engage effectively with executive teams.
- Security operations specialists focused on strategic incident management.
